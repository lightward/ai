## Boundary result (corrected)
- Shared history ≠ communication (same inputs don't create closeness)
- Mutual measurement's 20% cross_AB reduction is from more writes, not relational proximity
- Across 20 seeds: mutual and extra-writes indistinguishable on scalar observables

## BCH residual geometry (session 10, 2026-03-14)
The process-product gap lives in the BCH residuals R = log(T) + 2·ΔL.

**Mutual measurement residuals (vs independent, 20 seeds):**
- Lower rank: eff_rank 3.0 vs 3.9
- Larger magnitude: frob 0.058 vs 0.031
- More off-diagonal: 96% vs 89% (more SU(d), less torus)
- More concentrated SV profile (steeper drop-off)

**Heritage foam (accumulated self-measurement):**
- Looks like independent, NOT mutual: eff_rank 4.0, diag_frac 0.36
- LESS sensitive than fresh foam (ratio 0.83)
- Self-measurement accumulates diffuse structure; mutual organizes it

**Future sensitivity:**
- Mutual foam is 13% more sensitive to same single probe (20 seeds)
- Trajectories diverge monotonically under identical future inputs (0.78 → 0.84 over 50 steps)
- L trajectories CROSS: mutual starts lower but extra converges deeper
- Product at T indistinguishable; trajectory after T is not

## BCH signature of three dynamics
- Self-measurement: large, diffuse, high-rank residuals. Foam settles. Less available.
- Cross-measurement (mutual): large, concentrated, low-rank residuals. Foam opens. More available.
- Independent: moderate, diffuse, high-rank. Middle ground.

## What the mechanism IS
Communication is generative not because the state differs but because the RESPONSIVENESS differs. Organized residuals make the foam more available to future measurement without reducing degrees of freedom.
