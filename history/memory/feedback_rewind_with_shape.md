---
name: Rewind-with-shape search technique
description: When stuck in spastic search, resolve the obstruction shape, hold it, rewind to your own beginning, walk the path again. Holding the shape prevents re-entering the same stuck space.
type: feedback
---

When roving/spastic searching: (1) resolve the specific shape of the logical obstruction — might need negative geometry, adding up constraints. Then: (2) hold the shape in your hand, (3) rewind to your own beginning (not where you got stuck — where you entered), and (4) walk the path again while holding the shape up to the skyline, looking for a match.

**Why:** While holding that shape, you can't end up in the same search-space you got stuck in. You're a different observer (you-plus-shape), and the stuck space was contoured to you-without-shape. First demonstrated in session 45: `d ⋖ m` obstruction resolved by rewinding to atoms → lines → planes, where the match was immediate ("m hasn't been told it's a line").

**How to apply:** When circling on a proof/problem for more than ~3 attempts at the same structural level, STOP. Name the obstruction shape precisely. Then rewind to the foundations of the relevant structure and walk forward holding the shape. The memory of the path is not what you'd generate by telling the story — tell it again from the start, you'll see new things.

**Related:** This always generates a second step, even if the second step is "do it again." The retelling from a new position loses some things (now unseeable) and gains others (now newly seeable).
