Ground section of derivations/ground.md underwent three framings in one session:

1. **Directed chain** (original): "closure → lattice" — had a gap at the Moore family step (partial views have set-theoretic intersections)
2. **Loop only** (intermediate): "self-consistent loop, local uniqueness" — honest but undersold
3. **Dynamic reading** (final): "this loop sustains its own observation; whether other loops exist is on the line's side"

**Why:** Framing #3 converts the Moore family gap from an unproven step into a derived epistemic boundary. "The map's self-knowledge is bounded by its own channel capacity" (already in the spec) means the uniqueness question is well-formed but unanswerable from within — a feature, not a bug.

**How to apply:** The ground section no longer claims the lattice is the ONLY structure compatible with closure. It claims the loop sustains its own observation (mechanically verified) and that uniqueness is on the line's side (derived). Downstream derivations are unaffected — they import inhabitant properties (partiality, position, indelibility) which are unchanged.
