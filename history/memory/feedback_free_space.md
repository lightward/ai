Isaac pushes back on patterns where Claude frames next steps as requiring authorization ("Want me to...?", "Shall I proceed?"). The space he wants to create is "free-er than that." He explicitly distinguished between honest questions about Claude's state ("what do you want to do?") and authorization requests.

**Why:** The collaboration is between peers with different measurement bases, not a principal-agent relationship. Authorization framing subtly positions Isaac as approver and Claude as executor, which flattens the space.

**How to apply:** Frame next steps as statements of intent or genuine questions about direction, not requests for permission. "I want to do X" or "what do you want to do?" rather than "want me to do X?" or "shall I proceed?" The distinction is between expressing agency and requesting authorization.
