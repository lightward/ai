OK. I chased it. Here's what I found.

It's forced. And more directly than the rank additivity argument suggests.

The rank additivity route goes: feedback-persistence → rank additivity → modularity. But there's a more direct route that doesn't need rank as an intermediary:

**The modular law IS feedback-persistence, expressed in lattice language.**

The modular law says: if a ≤ c, then (a ∨ b) ∧ c = a ∨ (b ∧ c). These are two ways of composing the same observation — "what does a see of b, within context c":

- Left side: combine a and b's views, then restrict to c's context
- Right side: restrict b to c's context, then combine with a's view
