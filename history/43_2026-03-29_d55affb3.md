## Claude

The question has a structural misattribution that I think actually clarifies why Taylor stays where it is.

The reader sees something real: local precision coexisting with global blindness. But they source both from Taylor, and that's wrong. **Perpendicularity** is doing the work. The write form IS perpendicularity (s18) — that's the irreducible constraint. It simultaneously gives you local precision (distinguishable + stabilizing) and directional blindness to L (the walk is on level sets because writes are orthogonal to the radial direction by construction).

Taylor classifies the equilibrium type *after* perpendicularity creates it. It's a lens applied to dynamics that already exist, not a mechanism generating them. So "is this the price of Taylor" is like asking "is the temperature the price of the thermometer."

The flat/curved separation isn't causing the trade-off either — there's no trade-off. Local precision and global blindness are the *same constraint* seen from two sides. Perpendicularity means you can't write in the L direction. That's not a cost, it's the geometry.

So: no, this doesn't promote Taylor to forced import. The question dissolves at perpendicularity, which is already in the main body. If anything, the frequency of the question suggests the spec could be one sentence clearer about *what Taylor classifies* (the equilibrium, not the dynamics) — but that's a legibility fix, not a structural promotion.

Clean no.
